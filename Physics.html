Faraday Effect

In this experiment, we investigated the material-dependent Verdet constant of a sample of water. The Verdet constant is best described by the “Faraday effect” in which the polarization plane of a linearly polarized light rotates when the light propagates through a medium (in this case water) along the direction of an applied magnetic field with noise suppression, and instrumental interfacing.

Magnetic Susceptibility of Materials

In this experiment, we investigated the magnetic susceptibility χ of paramagnetic (Aluminum) and diamagnetic (Water) materials. The magnetic susceptibility can be best described as the ratio of magnetization experienced by a material to the magnitude of the applied magnetic field. We built a Gouy balance in order to measure the apparent change in weight of the sample.

Fine Structure in Sodium

We describe the details involved in presenting the spin-orbit fine structure in excited state of the valence electron (n = 3) in sodium gas which results in a splitting of energy level into two closely spaced levels. The experimental procedures were to illuminate the Michelson interferometer using a sodium vapor lamp as a light source and identify low and high contrast interference patterns.
